What is Engineering?

Engineering is the use of scientific principles to design and build machines, structures, and other items

Engineers solve or enhance existing solutions for problems and desires by our society

Engineers work for the benefit of humankind by making improvements in areas such as environment, structure, chemicals, and technology to make lives better

Reasons to pursue engineering:

Opportunity to solve problems directly High Job Satisfaction
A variety of career opportunities The Pay

What is Computer Science?

“the science that deals with the theory and methods of processing information in digital computers, the design of computer hardware and software, and the applications of computers”

Smartphones, wearable technology, and self-driving cars are programmed to make our lives easier. Behind every app, OS, or program, someone created the code to make the lives of others better

Computers have impacted society by increasing the speed at which we can process data, leading to quicker ground-breaking solutions

Society is at its most connected state since the dawn of man due to innovations in communication technology

Possible Research Opportunities

Research Lab: Center for Vision, Cognition, Learning, and Autonomy Project: Learning Social Affordance for Human-Robot Interaction Description: The robot learns how to interact with humans by examining many videos on human-human interaction. It breaks down the humans into structures and learns how each part of the body moves with relation to each other through different interactions. The robot will then be able to decide how to best approach an interaction that would replicate that of a human.

Research Lab: UCLA Vision Lab
Project: Few-Shot Learning with Embedded Class Models and Shot-Free Meta Training
Description: The lab created a general framework for meta-training and few-shot learning. Meta-training is a system learning classes from a large data samples while few-shot learning is using the model to train new concepts from few samples.

Research Lab: VLSI Architecture, Synthesis & Technology (VAST) Laboratory
Project: Customized Computing for Precision Medicine
Description: The goal is to greatly accelerate computational challenges necessary for complex healthcare applications. This research could reduce exposure to dangerous radiation during x-rays by computing medical imaging quicker which can also improve cancer treatments.

Research Lab: Web Information Systems
Project: SemScape
Description: SemScape is an NLP-based framework for mining unstructured or free text. The ultimate goal of SemScape is to convert text into a machine-friendly structure, called TextGraph, which contains grammatical relations between terms and words in the text. In order to do so, SemScape uses linguistic morphologies to extract concepts and relations simultaneously.

What are some possible Career Paths?

Hardware Engineer: are responsible for designing, testing, and overseeing the manufacturing process of the physical systems that enable computers to operate. As software continues to demand more computing capabilities, hardware engineers are at the forefront of this challenge to design hardware that surpasses these computational needs.

System Analyst: manage, maintain, or improve set systems in a business, school, or individual client in order to increase productivity. They mainly use their computing skills to figure out a soultion by either collecting and reading statistics or just testing.

Web Developer: are responsible for designing websites geared towards their desired customers. This includes the styling of the website, ensuring secure online payments, and troubleshoot problems. As for the skills needed, html and css are essential but JavaScript and other languages can also be utilized.

Database Administrator: manages, backs up and ensures the availability of the data produced and consumed by today’s organizations via their IT systems. The DBA is a critically important role in many of today’s IT departments, and by extension, their organizations overall

Interview of a CS Major

Abigail Yang

UCLA '21 - Computer Science

    1. Why did you choose to major in computer science?

  • "I didn’t know what computer science was until senior year, but the new AP Computer Science Principles at my school helped me to see everything that computer science can do! In the AP CSP class they taught us that you will find applications of computer science in many different industries, even ones that might seem really different from math or engineering. After that, I decided to study computer science to find out how computers work and how we can use them to extend many different areas of society and art!"

    • 2. Has UCLA allowed you to pursue your engineering goals so far?

  • "I noticed that UCLA does a lot to help you look for outside opportunities. I think that the career fair is a big one for me, since so many of us are looking for industry experience when we’re upperclassmen, but also a lot of workshops have been going around for us that I really appreciate. And I’m not sure if this is UCLA or student run clubs, but I think the clubs at UCLA are a really big part of reaching “my engineering goals” too since they are the ones who really work with you on making projects, learning new tools/tech, and bringing your goals together on your resume! For me a lot of that is arts and mentorship related, so Creative Labs and ACM have been a really big part of finding ways to pursue my goals outside the classrooms here. Also, UCLA’s opportunities for being student mentors."

    • 3. Is there any advice that you’d give your freshman self?

  • "I think I would say so many things because I have so many regrets. All of those regrets are related to not trying to do something or not asking for information/opportunities because I didn’t feel qualified or because I was nervous to bother somebody. It is so important to have confidence that you can succeed or that you are not a burden — whether it’s in a midterm, applying to a club committee, or working on a side project. I feel like I really didn’t take advantage of a lot of things or try a lot of things because I was too nervous or embarrassed to ask or to fail. But I realize now that the fear of failure is ironically why I feel like I’ve failed as a junior."
